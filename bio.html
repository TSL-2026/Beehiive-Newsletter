Ghanshyam Acharya

Chief Executive Officer & Safety Analyst

The Aviation Journey (1977 – Present)

Safety in aviation is a dynamic discipline that requires constant vigilance and institutional memory. My career began in 1977 within the Department of Civil Aviation, managing high-altitude airports like Tumlingtar, Baglung, and Dang. This foundational experience in operational control during the analogue era has informed my current perspective on high-tech safety systems.

Executive Leadership

Currently serving as the Chief Executive Officer and Accountable Manager under the CAAN AOCR, I hold overall responsibility for the effective implementation of Safety Management Systems (SMS). My leadership across various airlines—from Buddha Air and Guna Airlines to Sita Air—has focused on the critical intersection of operational control, resource management, and regulatory compliance.

Human Factors & The AI Horizon

As a dedicated Human Factors Instructor, I specialize in identifying the latent conditions that lead to system failure. I am an aspirant data analyst and a vocal advocate for integrating Artificial Intelligence into aviation education. By leveraging AI, we can reduce the learning curve for crews and detect weak signals in flight data long before they manifest as reports.

"Safety is the presence of capacity, not just the absence of accidents. We examine the latent conditions of the system today to protect the flights of tomorrow."
